NJIT FAQ BOT

Responsive Web Application

Background

A virtual teaching assistant that helps manage questions that are received. Encourages students and faculty to participate outside of class.

Company

  • New Jersey Institute of Technology: Ying Wu College of Computing - Web & Information Systems Department
  • Summer 2018 project under professor Keith Williams

Team

  • [My Role] UX Designer
  • 2 Other UX Designers

Duration

2 Months

Tools

Adobe XD, Lucidchart, InVision, Sketch

Challenge

How can we lower the amount of similar questions asked by users, but also deliver a platform that will host a chatbot that can turn a conversation into an interactive website?

PROCESS

The Product

Chatbot

First half of the project, we focused in designing a chat interface that students will be interacting with when asking questions.

Interactive Site

In the second half of the project, we needed to design a responsive site that would transition the chat interface to a visual representation of the questions asked by the user. In addition, it would it include extra functionalities that are not integrated in the chat. Essentially, the end goal was to come up with a user flow that will be passed down to the developers of the application by the deadline requested by the professor.

How the Chat Interface Works

Our first task was to develop an interface that will be used when a user speaks with a chatbot. This would include the dialogue of how a bot will respond, commands used, and error handling. Together as a group we came up with a dialogue. I then created a visual diagram that lists down possible paths a user might take when asking questions to the chatbot.

When a user enters the chatroom and asks the bot “When is the final exam?”, the bot should then respond in accordance to the scenario its in.

Designing Chat Interface

Each individual was then assigned the task to come up with a design of how the chat interface will look based off of the dialogue. After sketching, wireframing, and a lot of googling this was my end design.

Interactive Site: Information Architecture

With the design of the chatbot finished, we still needed to plan out how the web interface will look in conjunction with the chatbot. We were given a guideline of how he wanted to pages to transition to one another. Below is the sitemap that we came up with that will be our base to our design.

Everyone was then given different areas to work on the project. For my portion, I was in charge of creating a responsive search result page.

Interactive Site: Search Result Page

Outcome

With working with two other designers, they too had their own way of organizing and designing their pages. In the end, our professor decided that we focus on my template for the rest of the project. After compiling our work together, we created a flow diagram made with LucidChart. As of now, our designs are being used in developing the application (9 undergraduates + 3 graduate school managers).
